About

I am an applied epidemiologist focused on generating timely, actionable evidence to respond to the unregulated drug supply and reduce drug-related harms. My research examines how the composition of the unregulated drug supply evolves over time — particularly fentanyl concentration and the emergence of novel adulterants — and what those changes mean for the health of people who use drugs.

Since 2018, I have overseen the collection and reporting of provincial drug checking data as Research Data Coordinator for the BC Centre on Substance Use's Drug Checking Project. I developed British Columbia's drug checking technical training curriculum and have trained and supported drug checking technicians across the province and North America. My work sits at the intersection of research and practice: study design, drug supply monitoring, and program evaluation, in close collaboration with harm reduction services and health system partners.
