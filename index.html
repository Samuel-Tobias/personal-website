About

I am an applied epidemiologist specializing in substance use, drug policy, and public health systems. My work focuses on generating timely, actionable evidence to respond to the unregulated drug supply and reduce drug-related harms. I have expertise in study design, drug supply surveillance, and program evaluation, with a strong emphasis on methods that can inform real-world decisions.

I collaborate closely with harm reduction services, drug checking programs, and health system partners to design research that is rigorous, responsive, and rooted in public health practice. Since 2018, I have worked with the BC Centre on Substance Use's Drug Checking Project. There, I developed British Columbia's original technical training curriculum for drug checking and have trained technicians from across North America.
